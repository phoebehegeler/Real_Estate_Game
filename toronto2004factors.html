Factors affecting Real Estate in 2004

                 


  • Economic growth: According to Statistics Canada, Toronto's GDP grew at an average annual rate of 2.9% during this period, indicating a strong economy.

  • Low interest rates: The Bank of Canada maintained low interest rates during this period, with the benchmark interest rate ranging from 4.25% to 2.75%.

  • Population growth and immigration: The City of Toronto's official statistics reported a population increase of 4.6% between 2001 and 2004, reflecting a growing demand for housing.
